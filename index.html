⬡ PLAYER ROLES

The Six Positions

Every player on the court has a defined role. Learn what makes each position unique.

🏐
Setter
The quarterback of volleyball. Sets the ball for attackers, controls offense, and reads the defense on every play.
💥
Outside Hitter
Left-side attackers who handle the most sets. Strong, well-rounded players who can hit, pass, and serve aggressively.
⚡
Opposite
Right-side hitter who attacks from the back row and often serves as the team's top scorer and blocker.
🛡️
Middle Blocker
The wall at the net. Quick blockers who shut down the opponent's attack and run fast middle offense.
🐾
Libero
Defensive specialist in a distinct jersey. Cannot attack above the net but is the best passer and digger on the team.
🎯
Defensive Specialist
Serves, passes, and digs from the back row. Substituted for weak-serving players in critical rotations.
📋 OFFICIAL RULES

Rules of the Game

From the official FIVB rulebook — learn the laws that govern the sport.

Matches are played best of 5 sets. Sets 1–4 are played to 25 points with a minimum 2-point lead. The deciding 5th set is played to 15 points. A point is scored on every rally. The team that wins the rally scores a point and serves next.
The server must serve from behind the end line within 8 seconds of the referee's whistle. The ball must clear the net and land in the opponent's court. Foot faults, serving outside the sideline, or hitting the net are faults.
Each team is allowed a maximum of 3 contacts to return the ball over the net. A player may not hit the ball twice consecutively except on a block attempt. The ball may touch the net during play as long as it crosses completely into the opponent's court.
When a team wins a rally after the opponent served, all 6 players rotate one position clockwise. Each team may make up to 6 regular substitutions per set. The libero can replace any back-row player without counting toward substitution limits.
Common violations include: touching the net, stepping over the center line under the net, back-row players attacking from in front of the attack line, and double contacts (except on the first team touch). All faults result in a point for the opposing team.
The libero wears a contrasting uniform and cannot serve, block, or complete an attack hit from above the top of the net. They can only be replaced by the player they substituted. Libero replacements do not count as regular substitutions.

📅 TIMELINE

History of Volleyball

From a YMCA gym in Massachusetts to the global Olympic stage — the incredible journey of volleyball.

1895

Mintonette is Born

William G. Morgan invents volleyball at the Holyoke YMCA in Massachusetts as a less-intense alternative to basketball.

1900

Special Ball Created

A specially designed volleyball is created by A.G. Spalding & Bros., replacing the basketball bladder originally used.

1916

The Set & Spike

The Philippines introduce the "set and spike" offensive strategy, transforming volleyball into a faster, more exciting game.

1947

FIVB Founded

The Fédération Internationale de Volleyball (FIVB) is established in Paris, France, providing international governance.

1964

Olympic Debut

Volleyball makes its Olympic debut at the Tokyo Games, with the Soviet Union winning both men's and women's gold.

1996

Beach Volleyball Goes Olympic

Beach volleyball is introduced at the Atlanta Olympics, with Brazil winning the inaugural women's and men's gold medals.

1998

Libero Position Added

FIVB introduces the libero defensive specialist, revolutionizing the back-row game and adding a new tactical dimension.

1999

Rally Scoring Adopted

The traditional side-out scoring system is replaced by rally scoring to 25 points, making every point matter more.

2024

Paris Olympics

Italy wins the women's volleyball gold medal at the Paris Games, and France wins gold in men's beach volleyball on home soil.
